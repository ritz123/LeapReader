How to use Leap reader

The key feature of Leap reader is that it gives you much the same capability as a physical paper-bound book: you can leap to another page or document for a quick reference while still maintaining focus on the page you are reading. That makes reading technical documents much easier.

Menu & layout

  • Tap ☰ (top-left) for Recent, Libraries, Export highlights & notes, Help, About, and Panes (split vs single).
  • On a narrow window with two panes, the menu also has View → Split / Tabs (stacked vs one pane at a time).
  • When only one pane shows, use the floating Left / Right pill under the title to switch slots.

Two panes & files

  • Left and Right are independent: each can show a different PDF or different pages of the same file.
  • Tap ⋯ on a pane (bottom-right) for Open PDF, zoom, library actions, Print (with or without highlights and note pins), copy, highlight, notes, and marks.

Recent & Libraries

  • Recent lists PDFs saved on this device. Use Left / Right to open one into a pane.
  • Libraries opens collections full screen. Open a document from a library with its primary control or title.
  • Every time you open a PDF, it is also added to an Imported library (created automatically). You can still use other libraries and remove items from Imported if you like.

Pages, zoom & scrolling

  • All pages are shown in one scrollable column. The bottom ‹ › bar and page field jump to a page (scroll into view).
  • In the ⋯ panel: Fit width / Fit page and − / +. Hold Ctrl (⌘ on Mac) and scroll the wheel to zoom.

Text, highlights & notes

  • Select text to open a floating bar with Copy, Highlight, and Note (no need to open ⋯ first). Highlight opens the color palette; Shift+click Highlight (here or in ⋯) saves with your last color. Ctrl+Shift+H / ⌘+Shift+H (last color) and Ctrl+Shift+N / ⌘+Shift+N (note on selection) also work when the page has focus and you are not in a text field.
  • The ⋯ menu still has the same actions. Note on the bar places a pin at the middle of the selection.
  • Place note (toggle): tap a spot on the page. A dialog opens showing the document name, pane, and page; type your note and save. Notes are stored on this device and reload when you open the same file again from Recent (or after the PDF is saved to Recent when you first open it).
  • Tap a note pin on the page to edit or delete, or use Marks (either pane) to see highlights and notes from every saved PDF — then Go, Edit, or Delete.
  • Marks are not written into the PDF file; they are stored in the app (browser IndexedDB, or on desktop in the leap-reader-data folder). Use the menu Export highlights & notes… to download a JSON backup you can archive or process elsewhere.

Libraries from a pane

  • Add to library… / Remove from library… use the document in the pane you last used (tap the pane or open a file there first). The file must appear in Recent.

Privacy

PDFs, libraries, and marks stay on this browser or device only; nothing is uploaded by Leap reader.
